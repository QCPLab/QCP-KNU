About

A translational pharmacokinetics laboratory built around quantitative evidence.

QCP Lab focuses on docking, ADME/Tox, LC-MS/MS bioanalysis, in vitro PK/PD, PBPK, population PK, and machine-learning workflows.

Research Scope

Core experimental and computational workflows.

Molecular

Docking and molecular interaction modeling

Protein-ligand docking and transporter/enzyme interaction hypotheses for ADME mechanisms.

ADME/Tox

ADME, metabolism, transport, and toxicity

Gut absorption, hepatic metabolism, renal excretion, and transporter-mediated tissue uptake are visualized as continuous drug movement.

Bioanalysis

LC-MS/MS and metabolomics

Ion transfer from autosampler through LC column to MS/MS is animated, with dynamic chromatogram and MS spectrum readouts.

Computational PK

PBPK, PopPK, machine learning, and deep learning

Population PK profiles, virtual cohorts, and covariates such as CYP phenotype, sex, body weight, age, and disease state are shown as interacting inputs.

Research

Research interests

The research-interest order follows the requested platform sequence.

01Experimental PK and ADME

In vitro and in vivo pharmacokinetic studies, metabolism, transport, ADME/Tox evaluation, and PK/PD interpretation.

02LC-MS/MS Bioanalysis and Metabolomics

Targeted quantitative bioanalysis, metabolite identification, bile acid and endogenous hormone profiling, and mass-spectrometry-based interpretation.

03PBPK and Population PK

Physiologically based and population PK modeling, DDI simulation, virtual clinical trial analysis, and translational quantitative pharmacokinetics.

04Machine Learning

Machine-learning and deep-learning workflows for exposure prediction, population variability analysis, and computational pharmacokinetic insight generation.
