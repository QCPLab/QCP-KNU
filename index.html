QCP Lab

Laboratory of Quantitative and Computational Pharmacokinetics

Bridging wet-lab pharmacokinetics and dry-lab prediction, we investigate how drugs move through the body by connecting molecular docking, in vitro and in vivo pharmacokinetic experiments, LC-MS/MS bioanalysis, PBPK and population PK modeling, and machine-learning-driven prediction.

About

A translational pharmacokinetics laboratory built around quantitative evidence.

QCP Lab studies drug behavior across biological scales, from molecular recognition and in vitro ADME systems to animal pharmacokinetics, LC-MS/MS quantification, mechanistic modeling, and data-driven prediction.

Molecular

Docking and molecular interaction modeling

Protein-ligand binding, pharmacophore interpretation, and transporter/enzyme interaction hypotheses for ADME mechanisms.

ADME/Tox

In vitro ADME and drug-like property testing

Physicochemical profiling, permeability, metabolism, transporter assays, and toxicity-relevant exposure interpretation.

Bioanalysis

LC-MS/MS and metabolomics

Drug quantification, metabolite identification, bile acid profiling, endogenous compound analysis, and kinetic interpretation.

Computational PK

PBPK, PopPK, machine learning, and deep learning

Model-based translation, population variability, DDI simulation, virtual clinical trials, and data-driven ADME/PK prediction.

Platform

A large-scale platform for drug movement, exposure, and prediction.

The laboratory combines molecular modeling, ADME biology, LC-MS/MS bioanalysis, PBPK, PopPK, machine learning, and virtual clinical simulation in one quantitative workflow.

01

Molecular docking and dynamics

Protein structures, transporter/enzyme interaction, ligand binding, and molecular-level ADME hypotheses.

02

In vitro ADME and drug-like property testing

PhysChem, permeability, metabolism, transporter assays, and hit-to-lead optimization support.

03

In vivo PK/PD and disease models

Exposure-response analysis in animal models, disease-state PK changes, and mechanism-driven interpretation.

04

LC-MS/MS and metabolomics

Drug quantification, metabolite identification, bile acid and endogenous compound profiling.

05

PBPK, PopPK, ML, DL, and virtual trials

Virtual populations, DDI simulation, population variability, and machine-learning ADME/PK prediction.

Research

Research interests

QCP Lab focuses on drug discovery pharmacokinetics, drug-like property evaluation, metabolism and transport, disease-model PK/PD, drug interaction, and LC-MS/MS-based drug and metabolomics analysis.

Drug discovery PK

In vivo and in vitro pharmacokinetic studies for new drug discovery and candidate selection.

PhysChem and ADME/Tox

Evaluation of drug-like properties for hit-to-lead optimization and early development decisions.

Metabolism and transport

Mechanistic studies of drugs, bile acids, endogenous hormones, enzymes, and transporters.

Disease-model PK/PD

Pharmacokinetic and pharmacodynamic studies in disease animal models and altered physiology.

Drug interaction

Drug-drug interaction and drug-endogenous compound interaction studies.

Metabolite profiling

Drug metabolite identification, profiling, and mechanistic interpretation of metabolic pathways.

LC-MS/MS bioanalysis

Drug analysis and metabolomics in biological matrices using liquid chromatography-tandem mass spectrometry.
